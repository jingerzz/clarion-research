What I Believe

IBM is the contrarian AI play for regulated industries. The thesis is not about AI hype — it's about AI governance necessity in sectors that cannot move fast without compliance guardrails.

The compliance gap: Financial services, healthcare, defense, and government cannot use OpenAI APIs due to data sovereignty, model explainability (EU AI Act), and audit trail requirements. IBM's Watsonx.ai + Watsonx.governance is purpose-built for this constraint.
Red Hat + HashiCorp: Red Hat ($14B+ revenue contributor) provides hybrid cloud orchestration. HashiCorp (acquired 2024, $6.4B) adds Terraform-based infrastructure automation — already the enterprise standard for cloud provisioning. IBM bought the hybrid cloud plumbing.
Governance wave on schedule: EU AI Act enforcement ramps 2025–2026. US federal AI procurement requirements expanding. IBM is the only large enterprise software company that specifically sells AI governance as a product. This is a 2–3 year patience thesis.
Cheapest valuation: 16.29× forward P/E — cheapest of the 5 names. ~48% upside to base case. High stated D/E (211×) is an artifact of aggressive buybacks reducing book equity — actual debt/EBITDA is ~3–4×, manageable.
