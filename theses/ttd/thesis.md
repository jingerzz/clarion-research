## Core Thesis

### What I Believe

The Trade Desk is the largest independent demand-side platform in programmatic advertising, trading 74% below its 52-week high because the market has decided Amazon DSP will kill it. That narrative is too tidy. TTD processed $13.4B in gross ad spend in FY2025 (+11%), generated $2.9B in revenue (+18%), and converted that into $796M of free cash flow — a 41% adjusted EBITDA margin business with zero debt and $1.3B in net cash.

At $20.51, you pay 11x trailing FCF for a business whose founder-CEO Jeff Green just spent $148M of his own money buying 6 million shares at $24.71 average in March 2026 — the largest insider purchase in TTD history by an order of magnitude. He already controls ~50% of voting power. He needed zero additional alignment. This was pure conviction. The stock is now 17% below his cost basis.

The bear case is Amazon DSP. It runs on a 1% take rate (vs TTD's ~21.6%), has Prime/MGM/Twitch inventory, and one auto brand shifted $80M from TTD to AMZN last quarter. That threat is real. What restrains it: (1) Amazon DSP is a walled garden — agencies with fiduciary duties cannot concentrate open-internet spend there, (2) TTD's 95%+ client retention has held through five prior "TTD killers," (3) the new Kokai AI platform repositions TTD where Amazon's inventory advantage doesn't apply.

The CEO's $148M bet at these exact prices is the conviction anchor. The balance sheet ($1.3B net cash, 12% of market cap) is the downside floor. The $500M buyback authorization (Feb 2026) is the per-share accretion engine. Growth will likely bottom this year — but the asymmetry is extreme.

### Why I Believe It (Evidence)

**Founder-CEO's $148M personal open-market purchase at $23-25 is the strongest insider signal in this portfolio**

On March 10-11, 2026, Jeff Green bought 6,000,000 shares of TTD Class A common stock for approximately $148M (weighted average $24.71) on the open market. Not an option exercise. Not RSU vesting. Cash from his pocket. He already owned ~50% of voting power via Class B supervoting shares — he needed zero additional alignment. The stock now trades 17% below his cost basis. Any investor buying at $20-21 today enters at a better price than the founder-CEO with near-perfect co-alignment.

Source: TTD Form 4 filings March 2026; SEC EDGAR

**$1.3B net cash on $9.6B market cap with zero debt**

FY2025 balance sheet: $658M cash + $645M short-term investments = $1,303M liquid assets. Total debt: $0. Cash represents 12% of market cap. A $175M tax receivable (OBBBA legislation) will arrive in 2026, adding to the cash pile. The balance sheet provides a hard floor on downside.

Source: TTD 10-K filed 2026-02-27

**Business fundamentals remain profitable and cash-generative despite deceleration**

FY2025: revenue $2,896M (+18% YoY), adjusted EBITDA $1,196M (41% margin), operating cash flow $993M (+34% YoY), free cash flow $796M. Gross ad spend on platform reached $13.4B (+11% YoY). Net income $443M (+13% YoY). Client retention above 95% for the 12th consecutive year. These are not numbers of a business in terminal decline — they are numbers of a business whose growth is normalizing from hypergrowth to mature leader economics.

Source: TTD 10-K filed 2026-02-27 → mdna/chunk1

**Buyback pace is aggressive and accelerating at these prices**

FY2025 repurchases: 26.2M shares for $1.4B (average $53/share). Diluted share count dropped from 496.1M to 475.9M (-4.1%). In February 2026, the Board approved a new $500M buyback authorization explicitly for the post-selloff opportunity. At $20.51, $500M can retire ~24M more shares (5.1% of diluted float). Combined with Green's personal buys, total insider + corporate demand is real money stepping in.

Source: TTD 10-K filed 2026-02-27 → mdna/chunk3

**Amazon and Google are explicitly named competitors — this is disclosed, not hidden**

The 10-K states: "We compete primarily based on the performance, capabilities and transparency of our platform as well as our focus on the needs of the advertising agency." Amazon and Google are explicitly named as divisions of large, well-established companies that compete in the space. The market's "Amazon DSP will kill TTD" narrative is extrapolating from a disclosed competitive dynamic that has existed for years without breaking TTD's client retention.

Source: TTD 10-K filed 2026-02-27 → business/chunk1

**CTV is the structural growth tailwind and TTD's strongest channel**

The 10-K describes CTV as a "generational shift from linear television." TTD is the only scaled independent DSP with direct access to premium CTV inventory (Disney+, Netflix, Max, Paramount+, Roku) — these streamers deliberately avoid routing inventory to walled gardens. CTV is the fastest-growing channel in programmatic: "Rapid Growth of CTV" is the lead positioning in the business description.

Source: TTD 10-K filed 2026-02-27 → business/chunk0

**SBC is high (17% of revenue) but the CEO Performance Option cycle has completed**

FY2025 SBC of $491M (17.0% of revenue) reduces true owner earnings to $305M ($796M FCF - $491M SBC). However, the CEO Performance Option was fully expensed in Q1 2026. Future SBC ratios should normalize toward 10-12%. Normalized owner earnings of $500-600M would improve the FCF multiple materially.

Source: TTD 10-K filed 2026-02-27 → mdna/chunk3

**Near-term catalyst.** Q1 2026 earnings (May 2026) is the pivotal data point. If revenue hits the high end of the 10-12% guide and client retention stays above 95%, the "terminal decline" narrative begins to crack. Even in-line results remove the "deceleration will accelerate" fear.
