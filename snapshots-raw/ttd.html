That narrative is too tidy. TTD processed $13.4B in gross ad spend in FY2025 (+11%), generated $2.9B in revenue (+18%), and converted that into $796M of free cash flow — a 41% adjusted EBITDA margin business with zero debt and $1.3B in net cash. The bear case is Amazon. The counter is Kokai, 95%+ client retention, and the same CEO who bet his personal fortune at these exact prices.

The CEO's $148M Personal Bet Is the Ultimate Conviction Signal

Jeff Green bought 6,000,000 shares for ~$148M (avg $24.71) on the open market in March 2026. Not options. Not RSUs. Cash. He already owned ~50% of voting power via Class B supervoting shares — he needed zero additional alignment. The stock now trades 17% below his cost basis. Any investor buying at $20–21 today enters at a better price than the founder-CEO with near-perfect information. In 25+ years of insider transaction research, this is the largest signal relative to position we have seen in any thesis name.

The Amazon DSP Threat Is Real — But Priced In

Amazon is a disclosed competitor (TTD's 10-K names it explicitly). One auto brand shifted $80M from TTD to Amazon DSP last quarter. But Amazon DSP is a walled garden — agencies with fiduciary duties cannot concentrate their entire open-internet spend there. TTD's 95%+ client retention has held through five prior TTD killers — Google, Facebook, Oracle, AT&T's Xandr, and now Amazon. The bear case compresses the multiple; the base case renders it false through Kokai and client retention data points.

11× FCF for a Business Converting $13.4B in Gross Spend

FY2025: $2.9B revenue (+18%), $1.2B adjusted EBITDA (41% margin), $993M operating cash flow (+34%), $796M free cash flow. Those are not numbers of a business in terminal decline — they are the numbers of a business whose growth is normalizing from hypergrowth to mature leader economics while maintaining world-class profitability. At $20.51 you pay 11× trailing FCF. The SBC adjustment (17% of revenue in FY2025) is the key weakness — but the CEO Performance Option cycle completed Q1 2026 and ratios should normalize toward 10–12%.

Connected TV Is a Generational Shift — TTD Is the Independent Gatekeeper

Disney+, Netflix, Max, Paramount+, Roku — these streamers deliberately route CTV inventory away from walled gardens to maintain distribution independence. TTD is the only scaled independent DSP with direct, governed access to all premium CTV channels. CTV is the fastest-growing programmatic channel globally, and TTD's position there is protected by the same structural dynamic that keeps agencies from routing everything through Google: fiduciary independence.
