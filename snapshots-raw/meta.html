What I Believe

Meta is the cheapest large-cap AI business in the market at 21.94x trailing P/E (16.66x forward). The market is underpricing three things simultaneously:

(1) AI ad monetization: AI-optimized targeting raises advertiser ROAS → pulls more ad spend to Meta → drives CPM expansion without proportional cost increase. Online commerce was the largest contributor to 2025 ad revenue growth (10-K, MD&A).
(2) Llama open-source moat: Llama's emergence as the enterprise open-source standard gives Meta strategic leverage and ecosystem influence without direct monetization — a call option the market prices at near zero.
(3) AI hardware form factor: Ray-Ban Meta smart glasses + future AR headsets as a Trojan horse for the next compute platform. The market assigns this optionality essentially zero.

Operating CF compounded at 28% CAGR: $71.1B (2023) → $91.3B (2024) → $115.8B (2025). The discount to MSFT/GOOGL is not fundamental — it's regulatory fear and capex sticker shock ($102B investing activities). When the capex cycle peaks in 2026, FCF normalizes sharply upward.
