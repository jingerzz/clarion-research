THESIS SUMMARY

On Running is a Swiss-founded premium performance running brand (est. 2010) that has grown from a niche trail running label into a multi-category platform with a real shot at becoming the next $10B+ sportswear brand. The investment case centers on four pillars: (1) structural category tailwind in performance running, (2) rapid margin expansion as the investment phase matures, (3) APAC and apparel as underappreciated growth levers, and (4) LightSpray technology as a potential gross margin inflection point toward 66–68%. The Q1 2026 record quarter — EBITDA margin +450 bps YoY to 21% — proves the operating leverage thesis is working. At 16.4× forward P/E on $33.84 (near its 52-week low), the stock is pricing in execution failure while the business just posted record margins. That is a mispricing worth owning.

What I Believe
Premium Running Is the Right Horse to Ride

On is positioned at the intersection of the three fastest-growing segments in athletic footwear: performance running, trail, and lifestyle crossover. Unlike brands that chase trends, On built its brand around a proprietary technology story (CloudTec + LightSpray) that commands a real price premium. The running footwear market is projected to grow at ~6–8% CAGR through 2030; On is running at 3–4× that rate. Federer co-ownership and elite athlete sponsorships (Świątek, triathletes) anchor credibility in the core performance community while lifestyle extensions broaden the addressable market.

The Margin Expansion Story Is Real and Underway

Q1 2026 is the inflection data point: gross margin hit 64.2% (+430 bps YoY), adjusted EBITDA margin 21.0% (+450 bps YoY). This was an investment-heavy year, yet margins compressed the wrong direction in FY2024. The Q1 beat demonstrates that as revenue scales, fixed cost leverage and DTC mix improvement drive outsized margin gain. FY2026 guide targets EBITDA margin ~18% — even if we stop there, 18% on CHF 3.4B+ revenue = ~CHF 612M EBITDA. At current market cap, that's ~35× EV/EBITDA. Not cheap, but reasonable if growth stays above 20%.

APAC and Apparel Are Underappreciated Levers

APAC represents only 17% of FY2025 revenue — yet it is the fastest-growing region. China's running participation rate is rising fast, and On is early enough in that market that it is effectively a startup with a brand already built. Apparel is currently <7% of revenue, yet apparel at scale (as seen at LULU) dramatically improves revenue predictability and DTC margins. Both are binary options — either they accelerate and re-rate the stock, or they simply sustain an already-strong core business.

LightSpray Is a Potential Moat Inflection

LightSpray is On's proprietary one-step spray-on shoe upper technology — no stitching, no gluing, fully customizable. If it achieves production scale (Zurich facility opened 2025), it could reduce manufacturing costs materially and push gross margins toward 66–68%. It also creates a product differentiation moat that is hard for HOKA or ASICS to replicate quickly. Risk: scale-up has taken longer than hoped, and the unit economics at volume are still unproven.
