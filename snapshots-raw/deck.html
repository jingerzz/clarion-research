THESIS SUMMARY

Deckers owns two of the most recognizable footwear brands in the world — UGG and HOKA — and trades at 14.3× trailing earnings with 57.9% gross margins and 40% ROE. The market is pricing DECK like a seasonal fashion company. The data says it's a margin-expanding compounder with a second growth engine (HOKA, +23.6% YoY, $2.23B and accelerating) that the market hasn't fully priced. The core insight: DECK is systematically cheap because UGG carries a fashion-company discount — but HOKA's athletic/performance positioning makes the combined franchise far more durable than that framing implies. Prob-weighted price target: $163. Regime: ORANGE — size conservatively, add on dips.

What I Believe
The market is using the wrong mental model

DECK gets priced as a seasonal fashion brand because UGG has wool boots and a heritage in cozy weather. That framing was correct in 2010. In 2026, HOKA is 45% of net sales, growing at 23.6%, dominant in running and trail with a serious performance reputation. The combined entity is more like Nike at 1/3 the multiple.

The margin expansion is the tell

Three consecutive years of gross margin expansion — 50.3% → 55.6% → 57.9% — is not an accident. It's evidence that DECK is selling into its brand equity, not competing on price. Companies with real moats expand margins when they grow. Commodity brands don't.

HOKA is the next UGG — bigger

UGG built from niche surf accessory to global lifestyle brand over 20 years. HOKA is replicating that trajectory but in a decade. Performance running → lifestyle crossover → international — the playbook is running. At $2.23B growing 23.6%, HOKA is a few years from surpassing UGG as the primary revenue driver.

Management earns the right to own it

Divestiture of Sanuk. Wind-down of Koolaburra. Active buybacks. These are the behaviors of management allocating capital toward what works and cutting what doesn't. The 40% ROE is not accidental — it reflects a business running well and management not diluting it with distraction.

At current growth rates, HOKA closes the revenue gap with UGG by ~$250M per year. When HOKA surpasses UGG (est. FY2027–28), it will be nearly impossible for the market to maintain the "seasonal fashion brand" narrative. That re-rating event is part of the base case.

Why Now · Catalyst Rationale
Stock is 23% off its 52-week high

$133.43 → $102.72. The pullback from the high creates an entry that's reasonably priced for a quality business. Not a gift entry, but fair value on a conservative base case.

HOKA scale milestones — quarterly re-ratings

Each quarterly print where HOKA shows sustained >15% growth and UGG holds margins is a data point that chips away at the fashion-brand discount. No single catalyst — patience play over 6-12 quarters.

Tariff clarity could be a positive surprise

DECK manufactures in Vietnam and Cambodia, which are not the primary tariff targets. If tariff clarity in 2026 reveals DECK's supply chain is less exposed than feared, it's a near-term positive surprise embedded in the current depressed multiple.
